## Key Teachings

### Core Philosophy
**"No Flow, No Healing. Slow Flow, Slow Healing."**

Dr. Perry Nickelston treats chronic pain and inflammation through the lymphatic and vascular systems, not by chasing symptoms. Pain is a signal, not the problem itself.

### Lymphatic System & Pain
The lymphatic system is the foundation of healing:
- Removes waste and inflammation from tissues
- Supports immune function
- When blocked → pain, stiffness, chronic inflammation
- **Primary Issue**: Most chronic pain stems from lymphatic congestion

### The BIG 6 Protocol
A self-care lymphatic drainage technique targeting primary drainage points:

1. **Collar Bones** (terminal drainage points)
2. **Armpits** (axillary nodes)
3. **Inner Elbows** (cubital nodes)
4. **Abdomen** (cisterna chyli)
5. **Groin** (inguinal nodes)
6. **Back of Knees** (popliteal nodes)

**Method**: Gentle pumping motions to move lymph fluid through the system. Do daily for 5 minutes to boost circulation and reduce inflammation.

### Fascia & Chronic Pain
Fascia is the connective tissue web that surrounds every muscle, organ, and nerve:
- When healthy: Flexible, hydrated, allows smooth movement
- When unhealthy: Tight, rigid, restricts movement, causes pain
- **Root Cause**: Fascial restrictions create pain patterns that radiate throughout the body

**Triggers for Fascial Dysfunction**:
- Chronic stress (causes fascia to tighten)
- Dehydration (fascia needs water to stay pliable)
- Inflammation (stiffens fascia)
- Lack of movement (fascia adheres and restricts)

### Nervous System & Vagus Nerve
The vagus nerve is the master regulator of the parasympathetic (rest-and-digest) nervous system:
- Influences immune response
- Regulates inflammation
- Controls gut function
- **Key Insight**: Chronic stress keeps the nervous system in fight-or-flight, preventing healing

### Immune System Intelligence
The immune system distinguishes "self" from "not self":
- How you think about yourself affects immune function
- Chronic stress confuses immune signaling
- Lymphatic flow is essential for immune cell transport
